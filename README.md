# System-Analysis-and-Design

DriverPass project involved designing of a system for a firm that assists students to get ready for their driving test. This was an online system that would enable clients to sign up, book driving classes, practice taking driving test, pay, and monitor their progress. The aim of designing this system was to make it user friendly for both the clients and the staff.

Another area in which I feel that I excelled was that of determining the client’s needs and developing diagrams illustrating how the system will operate. Developing the business requirements document, the use case diagram, activity diagram, and the system design enabled me to have a much greater understanding of the interrelationship between the various components of the system.

If I had an opportunity to change something about my assignment, I would change my diagrams. Although they met all of the project criteria, I believe that I could have made them better organized and understandable for those who may not be familiar with the project.

When designing the system, I considered what the client needed from the application and how every feature would aid in meeting his expectations. Every design consideration was based on the findings of the interview and the business requirements, not on assumptions that I made myself. User needs should be considered in the design process because a successful system is one that addresses the needs of the client and the users.

In future, I will ensure that there is a systematic approach to the design of any software. I would begin with the collection of requirements, identification of both functional and non-functional requirements, creation of diagrams for visualization of the system and finally review of the design before implementing the project.
